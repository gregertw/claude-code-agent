## Quick Reference: Routing Decisions

When given a writing task, make these decisions in order:

### 1. Language

- <!-- e.g. "English for everything" or "Norwegian for local contacts, English for international" -->
- **Match the thread** if replying to an existing conversation.
- Never mix languages within a single message.

### 2. Formality Level

| Context | Level | Greeting | Sign-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| Known contact, routine | Casual | <!-- e.g. Hi [name], --> | <!-- e.g. Thanks, [name] --> |
| New business contact | Formal | <!-- e.g. Hi [name], --> | <!-- e.g. Kind regards, [full name] --> |
| Formal claims/institutions | Maximum | <!-- e.g. skip greeting --> | <!-- e.g. Regards, [full name] --> |
| Friends & family | Informal | <!-- e.g. Hey! --> | <!-- e.g. just your name or nothing --> |

### 3. Length

- **Ultra-short** (1–5 words): Simple confirmations.
- **Short** (1–3 sentences): Scheduling, simple answers, follow-ups.
- **Medium** (1–2 paragraphs): Requests, coordination, explanations.
- **Long** (multiple paragraphs): Claims, complaints, articles, detailed feedback.

Rule: Default to the shortest length that fully addresses the purpose.
